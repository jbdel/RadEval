# Plan 06: extend reward-divergence gallery with GREEN + CRIMSON

## Motivation

The benchmarks-page gallery currently shows 5 columns (BLEU,
BERTScore, F1CheXbert, RadGraph, RadCliQ). The README reviewer's
observation on the negation-flip row (row 3):

> "BERTScore gives 0.893; other metrics such as F1CheXbert and
> RadGraph correctly penalize" — **is this true? F1CheXbert gives
> 0.8 and RadGraph gives 0.50.**

The claim is overstated. F1CheXbert and RadGraph **do** drop
(ceiling is 1.0), but by very different magnitudes. Adding two
LLM-based clinical metrics to the gallery would:

1. Show how LLM-judge rewards react to the same rollouts; GREEN and
   CRIMSON both claim to capture clinical-finding correctness but
   via very different mechanisms (7B local LLM judge vs API-based
   GPT-4o-mini judge).
2. Give the reader a stronger version of the "which reward
   penalizes the flipped negation" question: across 7 metrics
   instead of 5, the relative magnitudes are easier to compare.

## Scope

**Experiment:**

1. Add `green` and `crimson` to `GALLERY_METRICS` in
   `examples/bench_rewards.py`. Rerun the benchmark against the
   existing 8-row divergence fixture.
2. Look at the resulting 7-column table, pick two columns to drop
   so the gallery stays readable (≤ 5 columns: 4-5 is the user's
   stated preference). Drop criteria below.
3. Update the divergence section of `docs/trl_rewards_benchmarks.md`
   with the new columns and re-evaluate which rows remain
   interesting (user's TODO: "pick most interesting 4-5 rows").

**Column-drop criteria** (evaluated on the measured table):

- Drop columns that are **redundant** with a remaining column
  (highly correlated across rows).
- Keep BLEU as the strictly-lexical anchor (even though per-row
  values are always ≈ 0; it's the floor baseline).
- Keep BERTScore (headline row's danger depends on it).
- Keep RadCliQ (radiologist-preference gold standard).
- Between F1CheXbert / RadGraph / GREEN / CRIMSON, keep the 1-2
  that provide the most distinct information. Criterion: sum of
  per-row absolute differences from the other clinical columns;
  the metrics that disagree most (= add most information) stay.

**Row-drop criteria** (also user TODO):

- Keep the exact-match ceiling row (row 1).
- Keep row 3 (negation flip, headline).
- Keep the most-contrastive 2-3 rows from the remaining 6.
- Drop rows whose signal is dominated by others' patterns.

## Risks

- **GREEN scoring per-sample is slow** (~2.2 s/sample on A100);
  8 rows × 1 sample = ~18s extra in the gallery path. Acceptable.
- **CRIMSON API cost**: 8 samples × 1 API call = ~$0.01. Acceptable.
- **Column-drop decisions are judgment calls.** If two metrics
  tie on "most distinct information," note both in the findings
  and pick based on secondary considerations (speed, clinical
  gold-standard status, etc.).

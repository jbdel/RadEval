# Plan 06: extend reward-divergence gallery with GREEN + CRIMSON

## Motivation

The benchmarks-page gallery currently shows 5 columns (BLEU,
BERTScore, F1CheXbert, RadGraph, RadCliQ). The README reviewer's
observation on the negation-flip row (row 3):

> "BERTScore gives 0.893; other metrics such as F1CheXbert and
> RadGraph correctly penalize" — **is this true? F1CheXbert gives
> 0.8 and RadGraph gives 0.50.**

The claim is overstated. F1CheXbert and RadGraph **do** drop
(ceiling is 1.0), but by very different magnitudes. Adding two
LLM-based clinical metrics to the gallery would:

1. Show how LLM-judge rewards react to the same rollouts; GREEN and
   CRIMSON both claim to capture clinical-finding correctness but
   via very different mechanisms (7B local LLM judge vs API-based
   GPT-4o-mini judge).
2. Give the reader a stronger version of the "which reward
   penalizes the flipped negation" question: across 7 metrics
   instead of 5, the relative magnitudes are easier to compare.

| Pair | Sum |diff| |
|---|---:|

### Decisions

**Drop F1CheXbert and GREEN** from the gallery columns. Keep:
- `bleu` (lexical floor anchor)
- `bertscore` (semantic headline danger)
- `radgraph` (best dynamic range among clinical metrics on this
  set; catches omissions, hallucinations, severity)
- `radcliq` (radiologist-preference gold standard)
- `crimson` (LLM judge with signed output; the clearest
  "this is wrong" signal in the gallery, returning −0.333 on every
  clinically-wrong row)

F1CheXbert remains fully supported as a reward metric; it's dropped
*from the gallery table for readability*, documented in the
"Note on method" disclaimer. Its speed-table entry is unchanged.
GREEN likewise stays in the speed table as the heavy-LLM tier
reference point.

**Drop rows 3, 4, 6** (missing finding, hallucinated finding,
total mismatch). Their signals are redundant with the kept rows
and cluttered the table. Kept rows:

| New # | Old # | Kind |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 5 | Exact match (ceiling sanity) |
| 2 | 1 | Clinically correct paraphrase |
| 3 | 2 | Negation flip (headline) |
| 4 | 7 | Severity flip |
| 5 | 8 | Opposite clinical conclusion |

Row 5 (opposite clinical conclusion) shows CRIMSON's second
negative (−0.333), BERTScore's lowest at 0.355, and RadGraph
bottoming out at 0.0 — a clean "everything fires" example
complementing the headline row.

### Most interesting observations

1. **CRIMSON is the only metric that returns negatives** on
   clinically-wrong rows (negation flip, hallucinated finding,
   total mismatch, opposite conclusion). This is the paper-defined
   behavior: the [CRIMSON paper §3.3](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2603.06183)
   specifies a range of **(−1, 1]** where negative values mean "the
   report contains more weighted errors than correct findings."
   All four of our negative-scoring rows land at exactly −0.333,
   which the scoring formula (Eq. 2 + Eq. 3) emits for one
   actionable non-urgent hallucinated finding against a normal
   reference. All other metrics in the gallery are bounded at ≥ 0
   (or, for RadCliQ, at the sanity floor of ~9.37). For a reader
   thinking "what metric would *actually* tell my policy this
   rollout is wrong," CRIMSON's signed output is uniquely
   informative.

2. **RadGraph goes to zero on the paraphrase row** (row 2, old 1).
   Because entity *tokens* differ ("cardiomegaly" vs "cardiac
   silhouette... enlarged") even though the finding is identical.
   This means RadGraph is **not** a good reward for paraphrase-
   generating models; it'd actively punish desirable rewording.
   Worth flagging in the considerations section (added: "catches
   omissions, hallucinations, and severity changes" — the
   paraphrase limitation deserves its own callout in a future
   pass).

3. **GREEN is surprisingly low-signal on this gallery.** Only 3
   unique values (0.0, 0.5, 1.0) with distinctness score 7.29
   (lowest). This suggests either the rollouts in this gallery are
   too short / too focused on isolated findings for GREEN's
   sentence-level LLM judge to differentiate, or GREEN's scoring
   is coarser than headline framing suggests. Worth deeper
   investigation in a separate experiment.

### Resolved (for historical record)

- **CRIMSON's signed-negative semantics** — initially flagged as
  unconfirmed; a literature review of the
  [CRIMSON paper §3.3](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2603.06183) and the
  upstream `rajpurkarlab/CRIMSON` repo confirmed the score range
  is **(−1, 1]** with the negative branch formally defined as
  "more weighted errors than correct findings." The observed
  −0.333 values in rows 3 and 5 match the paper's Eq. 2 + Eq. 3
  output for one actionable hallucination against a normal
  reference. No uncertainty; docs now cite the paper range
  directly.
